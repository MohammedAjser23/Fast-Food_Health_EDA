# 🍔 A STUDY ON IMPACT OF FAST-FOOD CONSUMPTION ON HEALTH — AN EDA 
---
## 📝 Introduction
Fast food consumption has increased significantly in modern lifestyles. This study explores how frequent fast-food intake may influence health indicators such as BMI and overall health score. Using exploratory data analysis techniques, this project identifies patterns, relationships, and potential risk indicators.

---
## 📊 Dataset Overview
The dataset contains individual-level information including:
- Gender
- BMI
- Weekly Fast-Food Consumption
- Physical Activity Level
- Health Score

Derived features created:
- Health Category (Underweight, Normal, Overweight, Obese)
- Fast Food Consumption Level (Low, Moderate, High)
  
---
## 🎯 Project Objective
- To analyze the relationship between fast-food consumption and BMI
- To evaluate differences across gender
- To explore physical activity impact on health
- To identify correlations between numerical health variables

---
## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Python** (Pandas, NumPy)
* **Visualizations:** Seaborn, Matplotlib
* **Environment:** Google Colab

---
## 📈 Visualizations Included
1. Univariate distributions 
2. Bivariate analysis 
3. Multivariate Correlation Heatmaps

---
## 🔍 Key Insights
- Higher fast-food intake shows a positive relationship with BMI.
- Lower physical activity is associated with higher BMI levels.
- Gender-based differences exist in average BMI.
- Health Score tends to decline with increased fast-food frequency.

---
## 💡 Conclusion
The analysis suggests that frequent fast-food consumption combined with low physical activity may contribute to higher BMI and reduced health scores. While this is an exploratory study, the observed trends highlight the importance of balanced dietary habits and lifestyle awareness.
